2. 5G Private Network

A 5G private network is a dedicated wireless infrastructure designed for organizations, offering enhanced security, high-speed connectivity, and low latency tailored to their specific needs. By leveraging the advanced capabilities of 5G technology, these networks ensure reliable performance for critical applications such as IoT, automation, and real-time data processing. Ideal for industries like manufacturing, healthcare, and logistics, 5G private networks provide the flexibility and scalability necessary for businesses to innovate and operate efficiently while maintaining control over their data and resources.

Applications of 5g

  • ● Vehicle accident prevention (V2V)
  • ● Reducing traffic congestion (V2I)
  • ● Automatic meter reading (AMR)
  • ● Crime reduction (Smart City)
  • ● Increasing manufacturing efficiency (iiot)
  • ● Remote robotics surgery (healthcare)
  • ● Massive machine-type communication (mmtc)
  • ● Connection with more IoT devices
  • ● Revolutionize public safety

7. Rno-Rnt (RNOP-RADIO Network Optimization Planning)

A comprehensive approach that combines Radio Network Optimization (RNO) and Radio Network Testing (RNT) to enhance the performance, coverage, and capacity of a mobile network. RNO involves fine-tuning network parameters such as signal strength, frequency allocation, and interference management, while RNT gathers real-world data through tests to assess network quality and user experience. Together, RNOP ensures that the network operates efficiently, meeting performance standards and optimizing resource use to provide reliable, high-quality connectivity.

8. Telecom Infrastructure (TI) & Microwave (MW) Rollouts

It involves the deployment of essential network components to expand and enhance telecom services. TI includes the installation of cell towers, base stations, and fiber optics to form the backbone of the network. MW Rollouts focus on deploying microwave radio links for wireless backhaul, connecting remote areas or augmenting the network. Together, they ensure reliable, scalable connectivity and support growing data demands across vast regions.

9. Operation & Maintenance (O&M)

Professional Services involves the ongoing management and optimization of telecom networks to ensure they operate efficiently and meet performance standards. This includes regular monitoring, troubleshooting, software updates, fault management, and capacity planning to minimize downtime and ensure high-quality service delivery.

Logistics refers to the support infrastructure required to manage the physical aspects of network operations, such as equipment procurement, transportation, warehousing, and inventory management. Together, O&M professional services and logistics ensure that telecom networks remain reliable, well-maintained, and capable of scaling to meet evolving demands
